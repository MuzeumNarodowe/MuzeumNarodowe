About museum

The National Museum is one of the most important museums in the country, known for its rich collection of art and history. The National Museum in Lublin is one of the most important museums in Poland. It was founded in 1879 and is located in the center of Lublin. It has one of the largest and most valuable art collections in Central Europe. This collection includes art from prehistoric to contemporary, including painting, sculpture, graphics, numismatics and ethnographic collections. The National Museum in Lublin has several branches, including the Gallery of Polish Painting of the 19th century, the Numismatic Gallery and the Gallery of Sculpture of Central and Eastern Europe. The museum also has exhibition halls dedicated to European and world art, as well as numerous temporary exhibitions. The National Museum in Lublin is open to visitors from Tuesday to Sunday, except for national holidays. Admission to the museum is free for people under 18 and for holders of valid IDs. The National Museum in Lublin is one of the most important scientific and cultural centers in the country, offering valuable works of art and rich history of Polish culture.
